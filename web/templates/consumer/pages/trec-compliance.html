4. Information About Brokerage Services

Types of Representation

Under Texas law, real estate brokers can represent buyers, sellers, landlords, or tenants in various capacities:

Landlord's Agent (Lessor's Agent)

A landlord's agent has fiduciary duties to the property owner, including:

  • Acting in the landlord's best interests
  • Following the landlord's lawful instructions
  • Disclosing material information to the landlord
  • Maintaining confidentiality of the landlord's information

Tenant's Agent (Lessee's Agent)

A tenant's agent has fiduciary duties to the tenant, including:

  • Acting in the tenant's best interests
  • Following the tenant's lawful instructions
  • Disclosing material information to the tenant
  • Maintaining confidentiality of the tenant's information

Intermediary

In some situations, a broker may act as an intermediary between parties. An intermediary:

  • Does not represent either party exclusively
  • Must treat all parties honestly and fairly
  • May be appointed to advise parties separately
  • Cannot disclose confidential information without consent

Important: Our primary business model involves representing property owners (landlords). When working with us, you should understand that we typically act as the landlord's agent unless otherwise agreed in writing.

6. Filing Complaints

If you believe that a Texas real estate license holder has violated TREC rules or acted improperly, you have the right to file a complaint with TREC.

How to File a Complaint

  • Online: Visit www.trec.texas.gov and use the online complaint form
  • By Mail: Download and mail a complaint form to TREC
  • By Phone: Call 1-800-250-8732 for information

What to Include in Your Complaint

  • Your contact information
  • The license holder's name and license number
  • A detailed description of the incident
  • Dates, times, and locations of relevant events
  • Names of witnesses
  • Copies of relevant documents
